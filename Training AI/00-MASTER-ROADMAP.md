## Which Path For Which Job?

"AI" is not one job. The role you target changes what to emphasize.

| Target Role | Must-Have Modules | Emphasize |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **ML Engineer** | 1-8, 11, 16, 17 | Coding + production + system design |
| **Data Scientist** | 1-5, 9, 10, 12 | Statistics, ML, communication, domain |
| **AI/LLM Application Engineer** | 1-3, 7, 8, 16 | LLM APIs, RAG, agents, shipping apps |
| **Research / Research Engineer** | 1-7, 9, 12-15 | Math depth, reading papers, deep learning |
| **Software Engineer (AI team) at Google/Microsoft** | 1-8, **11 (heavy)**, 17 | **DSA is ~50% of the interview** + ML basics |

> **Honest note:** For a Software Engineer role at Google/Microsoft, the coding interview (Module 11 / LeetCode) carries the most weight. For a Research Scientist role, deep math and published work matter most (often a Master's/PhD). For ML Engineer, it's a balance of all three: coding, ML knowledge, and system design.

## Beyond This Course — Lifelong Learning

AI moves fast. Once you finish, keep growing:

- **Read papers** — start with summaries (e.g., "Attention Is All You Need", then newer ones). Use sites that explain papers simply.
- **Kaggle competitions** — real datasets, real practice, a public profile.
- **Build in public** — share projects on GitHub + LinkedIn (Module 10 teaches this).
- **Follow the frontier** — new models and techniques arrive monthly. Pick a few trustworthy sources.
- **Contribute to open source** — Hugging Face, scikit-learn, PyTorch all welcome contributors.
- **Specialize** — once you know the breadth, pick a depth (NLP/LLMs, vision, RL, or MLOps) and go deep.
